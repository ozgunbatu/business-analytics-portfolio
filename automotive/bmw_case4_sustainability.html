Case Study: Munich Plant Renewable Energy Transition

Project: Convert Munich assembly plant to 100% renewable electricity by 2026

Approach: 50MW rooftop solar (30% of needs), 70% from long-term wind PPA (North Sea offshore)

Impact: 285K tonnes CO₂ avoided annually, €8.2M energy cost savings (vs. grid electricity), ROI 7.2 years

Replication: Model applied to all 30 BMW production sites globally by 2030

Material-Specific Initiatives

Aluminum (High-Carbon Material):

• Current: 8.5 tonnes CO₂/tonne aluminum (smelting energy-intensive)
• Target: 2 tonnes CO₂/tonne by 2030 (low-carbon smelting + 50% recycled content)
• Savings: 1.8M tonnes CO₂ annually (28% of aluminum footprint)
Battery Materials (Lithium, Cobalt, Nickel):

• Current: 60% of EV carbon footprint from battery production
• Target: 40% reduction via renewable-powered refineries, ethical sourcing
• Partnership: Direct contracts with mines using solar/hydro power
Steel (Largest Volume Material):

• Current: 1.9 tonnes CO₂/tonne steel (coal-based blast furnaces)
• Target: 0.4 tonnes CO₂/tonne by 2035 (green hydrogen DRI steel)
• Investment: €850M in steel supplier partnerships (H2 Green Steel, SSAB)

Business Rationale: Decarbonization isn't altruism—it's competitive necessity. EU regulations mandate 55% emission cuts by 2030 (or face €95/tonne CO₂ penalties). Customer preferences shift toward sustainable brands (62% of premium buyers prioritize ESG). Early movers gain cost advantages (renewable energy cheaper than fossil by 2028), attract talent (Gen Z demands climate action), and secure long-term market access (ICE bans spreading globally).
